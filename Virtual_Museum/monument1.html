Jean de Valette
(1495-1568)

The Jean de Valette monument was erected as a tribute to Jean Parisot de Valette, a French nobleman and military commander who ruled as the Order of Saint John's Grand Master from 1557 until his death in 1568.

The monument was designed by local artist Joseph Chetcuti and unveiled in 2012. The statue is 2.5m high. In the statue La Valette is shown wearing the typical knightly equipment and holding Valletta's plan in one hand and a sword in the other.

The Jean de Valette monument is regarded as one of Valletta's most significant landmarks that represents Maltese identity and pride.It serves as a reminder of the bravery and sacrifice made by the Knights Hospitaller as they defended Malta from the Ottoman Empire in 1565 as well as of Jean de LaVallette's lasting influence as a national hero and a representation of the country's resilience and independence.
